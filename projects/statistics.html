Statistical Analysis of Migration and Housing in Portugal (2025)

R | Grade: 14.6e

In this research project, I explored the relationship between immigration, GDP, housing prices, and emigration in Portugal. Using Panel Data from six countries over five years (2018â€“2022), we applied econometric models including Fixed Effects and Random Effects, supported by statistical tests like the Hausman test and Durbin-Watson statistic.

Key findings include a significant positive link between immigration and emigration, and a surprising negative relationship between housing prices and emigration. These insights suggest that migration decisions may be influenced by perceived competition and complex economic conditions. The project deepened my understanding of econometric modeling and real-world policy applications.
