As part of this project, we developed a data-driven Customer Segmentation strategy for Hotel H, leveraging nearly 112,000 records collected over three years and ten months. The primary goal was to uncover distinct customer segments to support personalized marketing strategies and revenue optimization.

Using the CRISP-DM framework, we applied and compared multiple unsupervised learning techniques including K-Means, Self-Organizing Maps (SOM), and DBSCAN. While Principal Component Analysis (PCA) was considered for dimensionality reduction, it was ultimately excluded to preserve feature interpretability for business insights.

The final model identified four meaningful customer clusters using K-Means, each with unique behavioral and demographic traits. Based on these segments, we proposed targeted marketing strategies to enhance customer retention and acquisition. The project also included the design of development and maintenance plans for future model scalability and improvements.
